## Grammar

**Present Simple — Full Practice**

### Structure

| | Positive | Negative | Question |
|---|----------|----------|----------|
| I / You / We / They | I leave at 8 AM. | I don't leave at 8 AM. | Do you leave at 8 AM? |
| He / She / It | She leaves at 8 AM. | She doesn't leave at 8 AM. | Does she leave at 8 AM? |

### Spelling Rules for he/she/it

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs → add -s | leave → leaves, set → sets |
| Verbs ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o → add -es | brush → brushes, wash → washes, do → does |
| Verb ending in consonant + -y → change y to -ies | study → studies, carry → carries |

### Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Correct | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| She brush her teeth. | She brushes her teeth. | Missing -es after -sh |
| He don't leave at 7 AM. | He doesn't leave at 7 AM. | Use doesn't for he/she/it |
| Do she come home early? | Does she come home early? | Use Does for he/she/it |

## Speaking Challenge

Talk about your daily routine for 1–2 minutes.

**Questions to help you:**
- What time do you wake up?
- What do you do after you wake up?
- What time do you leave the house?
- What do you do in the afternoon?
- What do you do in the evening?
- What time do you go to bed?

**Helpful vocabulary:**
first, then, after that, before, always, usually, sometimes, never

**Sample outline:**
1. Morning (wake up → brush teeth → get dressed → have breakfast → leave)
2. Afternoon (have lunch → come home → do homework)
3. Evening (have dinner → wash dishes → read a book → set alarm → go to bed)

*Try to speak without reading. Use full sentences in Present Simple.*
